My Story

I've always admired seeing other people working hard to make their art a business, but I didn't think I was up to snuff. With Imposter's Syndrome rearing its ugly head, I tried hard to find a day job I cared about. Call centers, vet clinics, warehouse jobs. Nothing stuck.

During the pandemic, I fell ill with Covid Longhaul, a post viral condition that tears the body's systems apart. I was devastated. I was too sick to work- disabled at 25.

Sickness has a way of changing your perspective. I was alive, after all. And if I was living, I was creating.

Due to stubborness, support, and time, I am finally back to a sort of normal. I went back to school for web design. I've sold at huge events in other states. I work with an animal shelter. I got married!

I currently spend my time drawing wacky characters and animals as subjects, along with a healthy dose of vintage style. My love of steampunk is why I also journeyed into web design. I love seeing how things work, with a strong emphasis on aesthetics. With my bold linework and colors, my goal in everything I do is to make people smile. I also enjoy lifting others up. Whether it is through eyecatching merchandise or a well designed webpage, I love collaboration. If my work helps you acheive your goals of being seen in the world, I've done my job.
